## Edge Distance Effects

### Reduction Factors for Tension

Reduction factor ψ<sub>s,N</sub> for edge distance:

| c/c<sub>cr,N</sub> | ψ<sub>s,N</sub> | Capacity Multiplier |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 0.5 | 0.85 | 85% |
| 0.6 | 0.88 | 88% |
| 0.7 | 0.91 | 91% |
| 0.8 | 0.94 | 94% |
| 0.9 | 0.97 | 97% |
| ≥1.0 | 1.00 | 100% |

Where c<sub>cr,N</sub> = 1.5 × h<sub>ef</sub>

### Example: CPRO 52/34

c<sub>cr,N</sub> = 1.5 × 47 = 70.5 mm

| Edge Distance (mm) | c/c<sub>cr,N</sub> | ψ<sub>s,N</sub> | N<sub>Rd,c</sub> (C30/37) |
|-------------------|-------------------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| 50 | 0.71 | 0.91 | 14.3 kN |
| 60 | 0.85 | 0.96 | 15.1 kN |
| 70 | 0.99 | 1.00 | 15.7 kN |
| 80 | 1.13 | 1.00 | 15.7 kN |

<Warning>
  Edge effects also reduce the projected area A<sub>c,N</sub>. These
  tables show only ψ<sub>s,N</sub> reduction. Actual capacity reduction
  is typically greater due to area effects.
</Warning>
